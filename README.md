## Overview
This project analyzes employee HR data to understand the key factors
driving attrition and workforce trends. The goal is to provide
data-driven insights that support HR and management decision-making.

## Business Questions
- Which factors are most associated with employee attrition?
- Do salary, job role, or tenure impact turnover?
- Which employee segments are at higher risk of leaving?

## Dataset
- Employee demographic and job-related features
- Includes age, role, income, tenure, satisfaction, and attrition label

## Analysis Performed
- Data cleaning and preprocessing
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Feature relationships with attrition
- Visualizations to highlight trends

## Key Insights
- Higher attrition observed among employees with low work-life balance
- Early-tenure employees are more likely to leave
- Certain job roles show consistently higher turnover

## Recommendations
- Strengthen onboarding and mentoring programs
- Review compensation structure for high-risk roles
- Implement targeted retention strategies for early-career employees

- Python (Pandas, NumPy)
